# Literature Context

Use this skill for literature review, background research, and source-quality critique.

## Search Strategy

- Start from the exact research question and define inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Prefer primary papers, official documentation, dataset cards, benchmark reports, and source repositories.
- Track search terms, databases, and date of search when available.

## Evidence Handling

- Separate direct evidence from plausible interpretation.
- Record negative evidence and conflicting claims.
- Identify whether a claim is recent, settled, contested, or speculative.
- Avoid relying on abstracts or snippets when the full source is needed.

## Report Expectations

- Summarize findings by claim, not by source dump.
- Include citations or links next to the claim they support.
- State confidence and limitations.
- End with the next decision the PI can make or the next question that must be resolved.

## Critique Focus

When reviewing, check for citation gaps, source quality, cherry-picking, outdated claims, unsupported generalization, and missing negative evidence.
